Dan Dan Mian

Dandan noodles or dandanmian, literally "carrying-pole noodles", is a noodle dish originating from Chinese Sichuan cuisine. It consists of a spicy sauce usually containing preserved vegetables, chili oil, Sichuan pepper, minced pork, and scallions served over noodles.This simple dish can be made easily in any kitchen for a quick and delicious meal any day of the week.

The following recipe for Dan Dan Noodles does not contain much protein, as is the case with many chinese noodle dishes today. Do feel free to eat with a side of protein such as chicken cutlets, or even sweet and sour pork. Here's a link to our Sweet and Sour Pork Recipe.

Ingredients

  • 3tablespoons Chinese sesame paste
  • 1/2 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 3 tablespoons black rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons light soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons dark soy sauce
  • 1/4 tablespoon salt
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons roasted Szechuan peppercorn
  • 1 tablespoon hot chilli oil
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • Dried chinese noodles
  • 1/3 cup roasted peanuts
  • 4 spring onions, finely chopped.

Steps

  1. In a small mixing bowl, add the sesame paste, sesame oil, and black rice vinegar. Whisk to combine.
  2. Continue whisking as you add the remaining sauce ingredients. Taste and add sugar if needed.
  3. Chop the roasted peanuts either using a food processor or with a mortar and pestle.
  4. Cook the dried noodles according to the package directions. Drain in a colander. (Do not rinse unless the package directions say to do so.)
  5. Place the noodles in a large (preferably warm) bowl. Whisk the sauce again and then stir it into the noodles.
  6. Dish the noodles into individual bowls and garnish with the chopped spring onions and peanuts.
