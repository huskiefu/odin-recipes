Sweet And Sour Pork

'Gu Lao Rou' or Sweet And Sour Pork is a dish originating from the Jiangsu Province of China. As the dish was spread throughout China and then around the world, every country has put its own ethnic twist on it. The sweet and sour pork we will be going through today is the traditional kind, which can still be found in almost every traditional chinese restaurant today, so popular that it has become a part of cuisine in areas like Singapore today. For those who cannot consume pork, we suggest substituting the protein with fish instead.

The recipe below comprises of two parts, the crispy fried pork and the sumptious sweet and sour sauce. Do feel free to taste and adjust the various quantities of ingredients to suit your own preferences. Sweet and sour may be something new to the western palette so we do suggest you brace yourself for the burst of umami you are about to experience.

Ingredients(for two servings)

Marinade
  • 200g pork, chopped into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon corn flour
  • 1 egg
Deep Frying
  • 1 cup corn flour
  • 2.5 cups oil
Sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 teaspoon garlic,crushed
  • 0.5 green bell pepper,chopped
  • 0.5 red bell pepper,chopped
  • 150g pineapple
  • 50g white caster sugar
  • 50ml rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine all marinade ingredients with the pork. Cover and leave to marinade in the fridge 20 minutes.
  2. Place the corn flour in a large bowl and toss the marinated pork chunks until liberally coated.
  3. In a medium-sized saucepan, heat the oil to 350˚F (180°C).
  4. Deep fry the pork pieces for about 3-4 minutes until the coating is golden brown and the pork is cooked throughout. Set aside.
  5. For the sauce, place a frying pan over a medium heat and add the oil.
  6. Sauté the garlic until fragrant, then fry the peppers until soften. Add Pineapple and warm
  7. Add sugar, vinegar and ketchup.stir. Bring the mixture to a boil
  8. Add the pork to the frying pan and coat everything evenely with the sauce
